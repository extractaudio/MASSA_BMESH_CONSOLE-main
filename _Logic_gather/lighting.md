# Lighting Reference Database

## 60W Incandescent Bulb

- **Watts:** 60W
- **Lumens:** ~800 lm
- **Kelvin:** ~2700K
- **Description:** Standard "Soft White" household bulb. Incandescent filament source. Omnidirectional hard light source (becomes soft with modifiers/lampshades). Very high CRI (100).

## Candlelight

- **Watts:** ~80W (Thermal output), Light output equivalent to ~0.05-0.1W LED.
- **Lumens:** ~12-15 lm
- **Kelvin:** ~1500K - 1700K
- **Description:** Extremely warm, amber light. Soft, flickering point source with very low intensity. Used for intimate atmospheres.

## Streetlight (Sodium Vapor)

- **Watts:** Common range 70W - 400W (e.g., typically 150W or 250W for residential/main roads).
- **Lumens:** ~15,000 lm (for a 150W HPS bulb) to ~50,000 lm (for 400W). Efficiency ~100 lm/W.
- **Kelvin:** ~2000K - 2200K (High Pressure Sodium - HPS), ~1800K (Low Pressure Sodium - LPS).
- **Description:** Distinctive orange-yellow monochrome glow. HPS has a broader spectrum than LPS but still poor color rendering (CRI ~25). Hard, downward-directional street lighting.

## Arri Skypanel S60-C

- **Watts:** 400W (Nominal), 450W (Max).
- **Lumens:** > 30,000 lm (varies by CCT, up to ~41,000 lm on newer Pro models).
- **Kelvin:** 2,800K - 10,000K (Continuously Variable).
- **Description:** Professional soft light LED panel. 115° beam angle (Standard diffusion). High CRI/TLCI (>95). Fully color tunable (RGB+W).

## Moonlight

- **Watts:** N/A (Reflected Sunlight).
- **Lumens:** N/A (Source). **Illuminance:** ~0.05 - 0.3 Lux (at surface).
- **Kelvin:** ~4100K (Actual reflected sunlight). Often artistically represented as ~5600K or cooler (blue tint) due to the Purkinje effect in low light.
- **Description:** Very soft, faint light. Creates hard shadows if the sky is clear, but ambient fill is extremely low.

## Sunlight (Noon)

- **Watts:** N/A.
- **Lumens:** N/A (Source). **Illuminance:** ~100,000 - 120,000 Lux (Direct perpendicular).
- **Kelvin:** ~5400K - 5700K.
- **Description:** Extremely bright, hard light source (approx 0.5° angular diameter). Parallel rays. High contrast with sharp shadows. White/Neutral color.

## 75W Halogen Bulb

- **Watts:** 75W
- **Lumens:** ~1,100 lm
- **Kelvin:** ~2900K - 3000K
- **Description:** Intense, crisp white light. Slightly cooler color temp than standard incandescent. High CRI (100). Often used in track lighting or recessed cans.

## 13W CFL Bulb

- **Watts:** 13W (Equivalent to 60W Incandescent)
- **Lumens:** ~800 - 900 lm
- **Kelvin:** Commonly 2700K (Warm White) to 4100K (Cool White) or 6500K (Daylight).
- **Description:** Compact Fluorescent. Diffused light source. Can have lower CRI (~80). Takes time to warm up to full brightness.

## T8 Fluorescent Tube (32W)

- **Watts:** 32W
- **Lumens:** ~2,800 - 3,100 lm
- **Kelvin:** Standard Office variants: 3500K (Neutral), 4100K (Cool White), 5000K (Daylight).
- **Description:** Linear diffuse light source. Standard for office ceilings. CRI varies (75-85 typical). Flicker can be an issue with magnetic ballasts.

## Computer Monitor (27" LED)

- **Watts:** ~25W - 35W
- **Lumens:** N/A (Source). **Luminance:** ~250 - 350 nits (cd/m²). HDR displays up to 1000+ nits.
- **Kelvin:** ~6500K (D65 Standard White Point).
- **Description:** Soft, area light source. Highly directionally controllable with privacy screens. emits blue-heavy spectrum unless calibrated.

## Green Traffic Signal (LED)

- **Watts:** ~8W - 12W
- **Lumens:** Intensity ~300 - 600 cd (Candelas) on axis.
- **Kelvin:** N/A (Monochromatic). Wavelength ~500-505nm (Green/Teal).
- **Description:** Highly directional signal light. Pure, saturated green color.

## Mercury Vapor Streetlight

- **Watts:** 175W - 400W
- **Lumens:** ~7,000 - 20,000 lm
- **Kelvin:** ~3500K - 5500K (Often has a strong greenish/blue-green tint).
- **Description:** Older generation street/industrial lighting. Poor color rendering (CRI ~45). Distinctive ghostly green/blue hue.

## Metal Halide Stadium Light

- **Watts:** 1500W (Common for stadiums)
- **Lumens:** ~110,000 - 150,000 lm
- **Kelvin:** ~5600K - 6000K (Daylight balanced for TV broadcast).
- **Description:** Extremely powerful, high-intensity discharge. Excellent CRI (>90) for broadcast. Takes minutes to warm up / restrike.

## High Bay LED Warehouse Light

- **Watts:** 150W - 200W
- **Lumens:** ~20,000 - 30,000 lm
- **Kelvin:** ~5000K (Cool White).
- **Description:** Bright, industrial downlighting. High efficiency (~140 lm/W). Wide beam angle (90-120°).

## Neon Sign (Red)

- **Watts:** ~3.5W - 4W per foot.
- **Lumens:** Low (~150 - 300 lm/meter). High perceived brightness/contrast.
- **Kelvin:** N/A. Dominant Wavelength ~620-700nm (Red).
- **Description:** Glowing gas discharge tube. Omnidirectional linear source. Pure saturated color.

## HMI 1.2k (Cinema)

- **Watts:** 1200W
- **Lumens:** ~110,000 lm
- **Kelvin:** ~5600K - 6000K (Daylight).
- **Description:** Hydrargyrum Medium-arc Iodide. Standard daylight fixture for film. Very high output, requires ballast. High CRI >90.

## Arri T1 / Tungsten Fresnel 1K

- **Watts:** 1000W
- **Lumens:** ~26,000 lm (Bulb). Focused beam intensity varies.
- **Kelvin:** 3200K (Tungsten).
- **Description:** "Baby 1K". Fresnel lens creates clean shadows and even spread. Focusable spot/flood. Standard warm studio light.

## Kino Flo 4Bank (4ft)

- **Watts:** ~300W (4 x 75W lamps)
- **Lumens:** ~3,000 lm per lamp (~12,000 lm total).
- **Kelvin:** Switchable lamps: 3200K (Tungsten) or 5500K (Daylight).
- **Description:** Large, soft light bank. Very low heat. Excellent for beauty lighting. High CRI.

## Redhead (Open Face 800W)

- **Watts:** 800W
- **Lumens:** ~21,000 lm
- **Kelvin:** 3200K
- **Description:** Open-face tungsten halogen. Harder, punchier light than a Fresnel. Spills light (requires barn doors). "Dirty" shadow.

## Match Flame

- **Watts:** <1W (Thermal output higher).
- **Lumens:** ~1 - 2 lm
- **Kelvin:** ~1700K (Orange/Yellow).
- **Description:** Brief, flickering point source. Very low intensity.

## Campfire

- **Watts:** varies (10kW+ thermal).
- **Lumens:** ~300 - 1000 lm (varies by size).
- **Kelvin:** gradient: 1000K (Red Coals) to 2000K (Yellow peaks). Avg ~1800K.
- **Description:** Dynamic, flickering light source. Hard shadows from a low angle. Warm, shifting color.

## Overcast Sky

- **Watts:** N/A.
- **Lumens:** N/A. **Illuminance:** ~1,000 - 2,000 Lux.
- **Kelvin:** ~6500K - 7500K (Cooler than direct sun).
- **Description:** Giant soft box. Heavily diffused, shadowless (or very soft shadow) light. Cool white/blue tint.

## Golden Hour Sunlight

- **Watts:** N/A.
- **Lumens:** N/A. **Illuminance:** ~400 Lux.
- **Kelvin:** ~3500K (Warm/Gold).
- **Description:** Low angle sunlight. Soft, warm, golden hue. Long shadows.

## Blue Hour Skylight

- **Watts:** N/A.
- **Lumens:** N/A. **Illuminance:** ~10 - 200 Lux.
- **Kelvin:** ~9000K - 12,000K (Deep Blue).
- **Description:** Twilight period. Indirect skylight only. Very soft, shadowless, deep blue illumination.

## Lightning Bolt

- **Watts:** Billions.
- **Lumens:** ~100,000,000+ lm (Instantaneous peak).
- **Kelvin:** ~28,000K - 30,000K (Blue-White/Violet).
- **Description:** Ultra-brief (<1s), high-intensity strobe. Extreme contrast. Bluish-white plasma channel.

## Car Headlight (Halogen H7)

- **Watts:** 55W
- **Lumens:** ~1,500 lm
- **Kelvin:** ~3200K (Yellowish White).
- **Description:** Standard automotive headlight. Focused beam pattern (cutoff).

## Car Headlight (Xenon HID)

- **Watts:** 35W
- **Lumens:** ~3,000 - 3,500 lm
- **Kelvin:** ~4300K (OEM White) to 6000K (Cool White).
- **Description:** High Intensity Discharge arc. Brighter, wider coverage than halogen. Distinctive bluish flicker at cutoff.

## Smartphone Flashlight

- **Watts:** <1W
- **Lumens:** ~40 - 50 lm
- **Kelvin:** ~4500K - 5500K (Neutral White).
- **Description:** Small, single LED source. Very hard light (relative to size) but low power. High Color Rendering (on newer phones).
