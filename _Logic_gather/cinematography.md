## Detailed Explanations

### Focal Lengths (Lenses)

* **Wide (14mm - 35mm):** Expands space. Close-ups feel intimate but distorted; wide shots feel vast. Used for both expansive landscapes and making characters feel small or overwhelmed.
* **Standard (50mm):** Matches the human eye. Authentic, grounded, "real life".
* **Telephoto (85mm - 200mm+):** Compresses space. Isolates subject from background. Good for focused emotion, romance, or voyeuristic paranoia.
* **Specialty (Tilt-Shift / Fisheye):** Alters the plane of focus or curvature of the world, creating a surreal or "miniature" effect.

### Angles

* **Low Angle:** Looking up at subject -> Empowerment, Dominance, Heroism.
* **High Angle:** Looking down on subject -> Weakness, Vulnerability, Diminishment.
* **Dutch Angle:** Tilted horizon -> Insanity, Disorientation, Supernatural unease.
* **Planimetric:** Perpendicular to background -> Artificiality, storybook feel, obsessive order.
* **Overhead (Bird's Eye):** God-like detachment, patterns, revealing geometry of the scene.

### Lighting Styles

* **High Key:** Bright, minimal shadows, low contrast -> Happiness, comedy, innocence, clinical sterility (if cool).
* **Low Key:** Dark, deep shadows, high contrast -> Drama, horror, mystery, noir.
* **Rim Light:** Backlight highlighting silhouette -> Separation from background, heroic profile.
* **Chiaroscuro:** Extreme contrast between light and dark -> Inner conflict, volume, classic art feel.
* **Practical / Neon:** Light visible in frame -> Gritty realism, futuristic/cyberpunk atmosphere.
* **Soft/Diffused:** Light through layers -> Romance, beauty, dreaminess.
